60's & 70's

Decades of Revolutionary Pop Culture Trends

MUSIC

In the 60's & 70's music was very much in a revolution and all sorts of norms were being broken. The genres of folk music, pop music, and "Rock N Roll" found their way into the mainstream media. The Beatles soon found their way to America in 1964 and stepped into the radical counterculture to bring the fans something revolutionary and thrilling.

Groovy Decor

Who doesn't think lava lamps are cool? These decorative beauties were designed by Edward Craven-Walker in 1963. They soon become all the rage and a very popular piece of home decor, every groovy pad had one! They were most popular with teenagers and house parties, their psychedelic glow easily allure you in and you soon found yourself totally lost in it's mojo.

Fashion

There was an abundance of cultural experimentation in the 60's & 70's, fashion being on at the forefront. Men and women were experimenting with very unique design trends. Generally young people were the ones who embraced the new styles, wearing bell bottom jeans, tie-dye, and batik fabrics infused with paisley prints. Hairstyles went from conservative to a more natural look. The decades created a new sense of freedom in what was fashionably acceptable.
