Maurice Sendak

About the Author

Maurice Sendak was born in Brooklyn, New York in 1928. [1]. He decided when he was young that he was going to become an illustrator. He went on to have some of his first works published in 1947 in a textbook. Eventually his illustrations would lead him to writing his own books.

After writing and illustrating Where the Wild Things Are in 1964, he quickly gained acclaim, making him internationally famous. Maurice continued to write and illustrate childrens books throughout his entire life. He also worked on production and design in television and even adapted Where the Wild Things Are to a playwrite. The New York Times quotes him as being "widely considered the most important childrens artists of the 20th century" [2].
