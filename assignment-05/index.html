Where the Wild Things Are

Where the Wild Things Are is a world famous classic childrens book that is one of my favorites of all time. An imaginative story told about unconditional parental love. The story is about a young boy named Max that causes havoc with his parents and is sent to his room with no dinner. In his room alone Max begins on a wild journey of his imagination, filled with adventure and lively beasts. On this journey Max arrives at his destination of where the wild things are, encountering scary beasts like creatures and unknown territory. He soon finds the courage to stand up to the beasts and is crowned king of the wild things. He playfully romps with the beasts through the jungle to finally send them to sleep with no dinner. Max eventually leaves the wild thing island to return back to his bedroom where he finds his dinner waiting for him, still hot.

This classic was written and illustrated by Maurice Sendak. It was published in 1964 and quickly became known worldwide. More about the author here.
